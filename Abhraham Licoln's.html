Abraham Licoln's Letter
to his Son's teacher

He will have to learn. I know, but teach him that for
every scoundrel there is a hero. Behind every selfish boy
there is a leader. I know it will take time but teach him
to learn to loose and also enjoy winning. Steer him
from every bad behaviour if you can, teach him the
secret of quiet laughter. Teach him if you can, the
wonder of books, teach him to laugh when he is sad. In
school teach him it is far more honourable to fail than
to cheat. Teach him to be gentle with gentle people and
be tough with the tough. Teach him there is no shame in
tears. Treat him gently, but do not cuddle him, because
only the test of fire makes fine steel. Let him have the
courage to be impatient but teach him to have the
patience to be brave. Also teach him to learn from
punishment and not repeat his mistakes ever again.
This is a big order, but see what you can do. He is such
a fine little fellow!!!
